Privacy Policy — PULSE

Last updated: June 10, 2026

PULSE ("the game", "we") is a free one-tap reflex game available on the web and as a mobile app. We built it to be fun and respectful of your privacy. This policy explains what we store and why.

What we collect

  • An anonymous player ID — a random identifier generated on your device to keep your scores together. It is not linked to your real identity.
  • A display tag — only if you choose to enter one for the high score board. Use any name you like; it does not need to be your real name.
  • Gameplay data — your score and the tap timeline of your run. The tap timeline is sent with your score so our server can replay the run and verify the score is real. We also keep aggregate play counts to see how the game is doing.

What we do NOT collect

  • No real names, emails, phone numbers, or accounts are required to play.
  • No precise location, contacts, photos, or device identifiers for advertising.
  • No selling or sharing of personal data with third parties. No ads, no tracking.

How data is stored

High score entries are stored on our hosting and database providers (Vercel and Upstash) solely to operate the game. Local preferences are kept in your browser or device storage.

Children

PULSE does not knowingly collect personal information from children. Because no account or personal data is required, the game can be enjoyed without sharing identifying information.

Your choices

You can clear your local data at any time by clearing your browser or app storage, which resets your device's player ID. To request removal of a high score entry, contact us below.
